Frequently Asked Questions — AI Photo Enhancer
Free image enhancement — no signup, no watermark, $0 forever
EraseAI's photo enhancer applies advanced unsharp masking, local contrast enhancement (clarity), color vibrance boosting, and intelligent sharpening algorithms — all running in your browser. Results are instant with no model download needed. Completely free, $0.
It depends on how blurry. Enhancement sharpens existing edge detail — it works well on photos that are slightly soft or low resolution. For images that are genuinely out of focus or heavily motion-blurred, sharpening can improve them but won't fully recover the original sharpness. Try the Strong level with Sharpen and Clarity both on for maximum effect, and use the comparison slider to judge whether the result works for your purpose.
Light applies a subtle pass — good for portraits where over-sharpening makes skin texture look unnatural, or images that are nearly sharp already.

Medium is the right starting point for most images. Product photos, landscapes, and general photography all tend to look noticeably better without going over the top.

Strong is for images that really need it — low-resolution photos, screenshots that have been scaled up, or images where you want maximum texture and detail recovery.
Good workflow for product photos: enhance here first to sharpen the product details, then use the Background Remover to cut out the background, then the Background Color Changer to add white. The "Continue editing" section that appears after enhancing makes this a one-click handoff between tools — no downloading and re-uploading between steps.
No watermarks, no charges, no account. The enhanced image downloads exactly as processed — full quality, clean file. Use it as many times as you want on as many images as you need.
JPG, JPEG, PNG, and WEBP all work as inputs. Download results as JPG for the smallest file size, or PNG if you need to preserve transparency or want lossless quality.
After processing, the before/after comparison loads automatically. Drag the center divider left and right to reveal the original or the enhanced version. It's the clearest way to judge whether the enhancement actually helped — you're comparing the same image at the same scale, not two separate thumbnails.
