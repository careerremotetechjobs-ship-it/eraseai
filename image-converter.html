Which format should you use?

JPG is the standard for photographs and complex images with lots of color variation. It compresses well, is universally supported, and is what most cameras shoot in. The tradeoff is it doesn't support transparency, and quality degrades slightly each time you save.

PNG is lossless — the quality never degrades — and supports full transparency. It's the right choice for logos, screenshots, graphics with text, and any image with a transparent background. Files tend to be larger than JPG for photographs.

WEBP is Google's modern format that beats both JPG and PNG on file size while maintaining quality. It supports transparency like PNG but produces files 25–35% smaller. All major browsers support it. If you're adding images to a website, WEBP is the right default choice.

PDF is the right output when you need to share an image as a document — for presentations, client deliverables, or any situation where you want the image embedded in a single-page document format that opens on any device.
