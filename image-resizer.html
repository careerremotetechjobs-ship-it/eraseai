Frequently Asked Questions — Image Resizer
Free proportional image resizing — no stretching, no signup, $0 forever
Switch to Fit Mode — it's the default setting. Instead of forcing your image to fill the exact dimensions (which causes distortion), it scales the image proportionally to fit inside the target size and fills the leftover space with your chosen background color. A portrait photo going into a landscape canvas will stay a portrait — it won't get squashed sideways.
Fit Mode keeps your image's original proportions and pads the leftover space with a background color. This is right for almost every situation — product photos, social media images, headshots.

Stretch Mode fills the entire canvas with your image, which will distort it if the aspect ratios don't match. It's rarely what you want, but it's there if you need pixel-perfect fill regardless of distortion.
Amazon's minimum is 1000px on the longest side, but 1500px or larger is recommended to enable the zoom feature on listings. Use the 2000×2000 preset with Fit Mode and a white background — this gives you a square canvas with your product centered and white padding filling any empty space, which matches what Amazon expects for main product images.
Instagram square posts are 1080×1080. Portrait posts (1080×1350) take up more of the feed and tend to perform better for visibility. Stories and Reels both use 1080×1920. All three are available as presets — just click and your dimensions update automatically.
YouTube thumbnails should be 1280×720 pixels (16:9 ratio). Pick the 1280×720 preset and you're set. One thing to check after resizing — YouTube requires thumbnails to be under 2MB. Run your image through the Image Compressor after resizing if the file is too large.
Resize first, then compress. Use the Image Compressor after resizing — it shows you the before and after file size so you can dial in the quality slider until you hit your KB target. This two-step approach is the most reliable way to control both pixel dimensions and file size independently.
Nothing gets watermarked, nothing requires payment, and there's no account to create. The tool runs in your browser, processes your image locally, and lets you download the result clean. That's it.
